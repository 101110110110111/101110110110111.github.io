Cristiano Ronaldo

Cristiano Ronaldo, typically known as CR7, is considered one of the greatest football players of all time. From cleaning the streets of his hometown to conquering the world, Ronaldo has simply done it all. A career so great he's only played for the best teams in each of the country's he's played in; including the biggest club of all-time, Real Madrid.

Club History:
  • Sporting CP (Portugal)
  • Manchester United (England)
  • Real Madrid (Spain)
  • Juventus (Italy)

Club Career

Making his professional debut with Sporting CP at the age of 17, Ronaldo hit the ground running, putting the world on notice of his immense talent. After a magical dispaly vs Manchester United in a pre-season friendly, the English club would go on to sign the young talent and make him their new number 7; hence the nickname CR7. From that point forward Ronaldo would go on to have a bright career achieving success at not just Manchester United, but other clubs like Real Madrid and Juventus. During his time with Madrid, he became the clubs all time leading scorer with a tally of 451 goals.

National Team

Cristiano Ronaldo obtained his first senior cap for the Portugal National Team at the age of 18. Instantly becoming an important piece for his national side, Ronaldo was named team captain only just being 22-years-old. In the years to come he would help his team win the 2016 UEFA Eurocup and the 2019 UEFA Nations League. Not to mention taking Portugal to a semifinal appearance at the 2006 World Cup, followed by a quarter-final appearance at the 2010 World Cup.
