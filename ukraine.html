Ukraine

During the 10th and 11th centuries Ukraine was the center of the first eastern Slavic state. Known then as Kyivan Rus, the most powerful state in Europe. The foundation for Ukrainian nationalism was laid by the cultural and religious legacy of Kyivan Rus.

Geography

Location

Ukraine is located in eastern Europe in between Poland, Romania, Moldova, and Russia. It is also bordered by the Black Sea.

People and Society

Ukraine has a population of 43,745,640 people. Amongst those people their are ethnic groups such as Ukrainian, Russian, Belorussian, Moldovan, Crimean Tator, Bulgarian, Hungarian, Romanian, Polish, Jewish, and others. The official language is Ukrainian, even though Russian is understood to be the regional language.

Most of Ukraine's population identifies themselves as Christian. Two thirds of those people identify as Orthodox, but do not belong to a specific branch. Other practiced religions are Muslim and Jewish, which only less than 1% of the population profess.

Government

Constitution

The current constitution was adopted and ratified in 28 June 1996. Law(s) must be proposed by the president of Ukraine or at least one-third of the Supreme Council. In order for these laws to be adopted, a simple majority vote by the Council is needed along with at least a two-thirds majority vote in its next regular session.

Election and Appointments

A president is elected by an absolute majority vote to serve a 5-year term. The Cabinet of Ministers is nominated by the prime minister, and approved by the Verkhova Rada (unicameral parliament of Ukraine).
